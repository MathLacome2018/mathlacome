## Question

Does a two-minute cold shower immediately before a writing block change how long I can sit and focus before reaching for my phone?

## Setup

Fourteen writing sessions over three weeks, alternating cold-shower mornings with normal-shower mornings. Same desk, same time of day (07:30), same 45-minute block. I counted phone pick-ups using the screen-time log rather than trusting my own memory.

## What I measured

Phone pick-ups within the first 20 minutes of each session, and total pick-ups across the full 45 minutes.

Average pick-ups in the first 20 minutes: 7.1 on normal mornings versus 4.6 on cold-shower mornings.

## Result

Cold showers cut early phone check-ins by roughly a third. The effect faded almost entirely after the first 20 minutes — by the end of the 45-minute block, totals were within noise of each other. Worth doing, but don't expect it to carry a whole session.

## What I'd change

Next time I'd test water temperature more precisely (I was guessing "cold enough to be unpleasant") and extend the block to 60 minutes to see exactly where the effect disappears.
