## Question

Does handwriting a short journal entry before bed shorten the time it takes me to fall asleep, compared with no pre-bed writing at all?

## Setup

The plan was fourteen nights alternating journalling and no journalling, timing sleep onset with a phone app left face-down on the nightstand.

## What I measured

Only got as far as: time to fall asleep (self-estimated on waking), and a same rough 1–5 rumination rating before turning the light off.

## Result

Abandoned after eight nights. The bigger problem turned out to be the measurement itself — checking the time and thinking about how quickly I'd fall asleep was keeping me more alert than either condition on its own. Whatever difference journalling made was swamped by that.

## What I'd change

If I revisit this, sleep onset needs to be measured without me thinking about it at all while awake — a wearable that timestamps sleep automatically, not a phone app I'm aware of. Also worth separating "journalling" from "screens off ten minutes earlier", since I wasn't controlling for that.
