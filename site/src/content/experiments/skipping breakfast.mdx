## Question

I'm a morning person by chronotype but usually my productivity is not optimal before 11 am and I need multiple coffee in the morning.

## Setup

Simple protocol - skipping breakfast for a week; checking results.

## What I measured

I tracked three things: subjective morning productivity, coffee intakes, and feeling of hunger.

## Result

After the first two days, feeling of hunder start to regulate well. I don't need any morning snacks and I'm not starving at lunch. I was also surprised by my willingness to eat less for lunch too. My coffee consumption moved down to (1 in the morning vs at least 3 before). Feeling is good. Of course I would not advise this for athletes. They need energy to keep their body moving. 

What could be the cause (acknowledging it is very individual): extending the overnight fast into the morning could force insulin stays low and ketone bodies rise slightly. Some people have experienced increased alertness and mental clarity in that state. My main hypothesis is that my breakfast was done with the kids - so mainly carbs. This is probably creating a rapid rise in blood glucose followed by a drop and the brain fog. But as the body adapt to this (partial) intermittent fasting, the body learn to delivery energy with more stability, probably reducing fatigue sensation across the day.

## What I'd change

I should have tracked glucose level across the 14 days experiment. First week as baseline and second week as test. Simple system like Freestyle libre could have make this experiment cheap.
