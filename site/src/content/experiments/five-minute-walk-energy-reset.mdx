## Question

At my usual 2pm slump, does a five-minute outdoor walk restore alertness better than a second coffee?

## Setup

On alternating weekdays I take either a five-minute walk around the block or a coffee refill at 2pm, then rate my alertness (1–10) at 2:05pm and again at 2:30pm. Still early — six data points so far, aiming for at least twenty before drawing a conclusion.

## What I measured

Self-rated alertness at two checkpoints after the intervention, plus whether I reached for a second caffeine hit before 4pm regardless of which group I was in.

## Result

Not enough data yet to say anything reliable. Early numbers lean slightly in favour of the walk for the 2:30pm reading, but the sample is far too small to trust.

## What I'd change

Nothing yet — still running. If the walk group keeps outperforming past twenty sessions, the next version of this experiment should control for how much sleep I got the night before, which is probably a bigger factor than either intervention.
